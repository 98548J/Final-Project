SPH Fluid Simulator

Prompt

Describe a topic, idea, or experience that you find so engaging that it makes you lose all track of time. What have you done to learn more or engage further in the topic, idea or experience? What resources do you use to learn more?

Essay

With anticipation, I execute my SPH fluid simulation; obediently, my program begins to process as I have commanded. Myriads of particles chaotically bounce around in the mathematical prison I have created. Unfortunately, this is where the fun ends. A few moments later, my program crashed. I'm trying to implement a GPU-friendly, regional sorting algorithm.


When I program, I'll frequently learn bits of information through quick Google searches. However, I knew this would only get me so far. As such, I have invested in programming books: three on Python, one on C#, and one on C++.


In addition to monetary investments, My involvement in competitive robotics has allowed me to flourish in programming. Through robotics, I learned how to use powerful tools such as Git Hub and VSC. In robotics, I get to collaborate with my team while programming. Because of this, I have realized the value of clean, maintainable code. Working in a team has also taught me the value of the engineering design process; documenting our work increased my productivity drastically.


I am prepared for this! After hours of debugging and hair-pulling, I realized the mathematical prison I created was not impenetrable. I've been allowing particles to escape my prison; these escapees are unsortable by my algorithm. I corrected my physics engine, the warden of my prison, by checking for escaped particles only after calculating their new positions. Confident I had eradicated the bug, I ran my program one last time.
